The United States has reefs across three very different worlds — the tropical
Florida Keys, the volcanic Pacific of Hawaii, and a surprising oasis in the
Gulf of Mexico. These ten are the headline sites.

## 1. Florida Keys Reef

The only living coral barrier reef in the continental United States, running
the length of the Keys within a national marine sanctuary.

## 2. Molokini Crater, Hawaii

A sunken volcanic crater off Maui with exceptional visibility and dense reef
fish — one of the most popular snorkel sites in the world.

## 3. Hanauma Bay, Oʻahu

A protected volcanic bay and the classic Hawaiian beginner reef, with daily
visitor caps to protect it.

## 4. Flower Garden Banks, Texas

A remarkable coral reef rising from salt domes 160 km offshore in the Gulf —
one of the healthiest reefs in US waters.

## 5. Dry Tortugas, Florida

Remote, pristine reef inside a national park reachable only by boat or
seaplane.

## 6. Buck Island Reef, US Virgin Islands

A national monument with a marked underwater snorkel trail.

## 7. Kealakekua Bay, Hawaii (Big Island)

A marine life conservation district with vivid coral and spinner dolphins.

## 8. Looe Key, Florida

A spur-and-groove reef in the Keys sanctuary, a favourite shallow dive and
snorkel.

## 9. Gray's Reef, Georgia

A rare live-bottom temperate reef and national marine sanctuary off the
Atlantic coast.

## 10. Channel Islands kelp reefs, California

Cold-water kelp-forest reefs of astonishing productivity off Southern
California.

## How US reef access works

Most premier reefs sit within National Marine Sanctuaries or national/state
parks with rules: mooring buoys (no anchoring on coral), reef-safe sunscreen
requirements, visitor caps (Hanauma Bay), and no-take zones. Use authorised
charter operators — they know the moorings and the regulations.

## Timing and conditions

Florida reefs are best in summer for calm, warm water but risk hurricane
season; Hawaii is year-round with winter swell on north shores; California
kelp diving rewards autumn's clearest water. Always check sanctuary advisories
and reef-health alerts before booking.

## Protect what you came to see

Reef-safe sunscreen or a rash guard (oxybenzone sunscreens damage coral and
are banned in Hawaii), perfect buoyancy, never stand on or touch coral, take
no shells or coral, and keep distance from wildlife. US reefs are stressed by
bleaching — low-impact visitors matter.
