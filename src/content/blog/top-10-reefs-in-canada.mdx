Canada is not where most people picture reefs — and that is exactly why its
reefs are extraordinary. Cold, clear, nutrient-rich water supports rich
temperate reef life and the planet's only living glass sponge reefs. These ten
stand out.

## 1. Hecate Strait Glass Sponge Reefs, BC

A globally unique marine wonder — reefs built by glass sponges, thought extinct
since the dinosaurs until found alive off British Columbia. Now a protected
Marine Protected Area.

## 2. Howe Sound sponge reefs, BC

Accessible glass sponge bioherms near Vancouver, a focus of conservation
diving.

## 3. Browning Pass, BC

Often called one of the best cold-water dives in the world — walls
upholstered in soft corals and invertebrates.

## 4. God's Pocket, BC

A remote northern Vancouver Island site famous for technicolour temperate reef
life.

## 5. Race Rocks, BC

A protected ecological reserve teeming with rockfish, anemones, and pinnipeds.

## 6. Flower Pot Island / Fathom Five, Ontario

Freshwater "reef" structures and shipwrecks in Georgian Bay's clear waters —
Canada's first national marine park.

## 7. Bay of Fundy ledges, NB/NS

Extreme-tide rock reefs supporting dense cold-water invertebrate communities.

## 8. St. Lawrence Estuary reefs, Quebec

Cold, productive reef habitat in one of the world's great estuaries.

## 9. Saanich Inlet, BC

A deep fjord with unique low-oxygen-adapted reef life and easy shore access.

## 10. Newfoundland coastal reefs

Iceberg-cooled rocky reefs and a growing cold-water diving scene.

## Why Canada's reefs matter

The glass sponge reefs alone make Canada globally significant — living examples
of an ecosystem the fossil record suggested had vanished 40 million years ago.
They filter enormous volumes of seawater and are extraordinarily fragile;
several sites are closed to bottom-contact activity entirely.

## Diving conditions

This is cold-water diving: drysuit territory, strong currents at the best
sites, and seasonal visibility that is often best in winter. Go with operators
experienced in Pacific Northwest conditions — these are advanced sites, not
warm-water reef snorkels.

## Conservation first

The glass sponge reefs are protected; respect all Marine Protected Area rules,
maintain perfect buoyancy, and never contact the structure — a single fin kick
can destroy centuries of growth. Choose operators that follow MPA codes.
