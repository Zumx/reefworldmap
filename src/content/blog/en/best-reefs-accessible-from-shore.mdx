Reefs vary enormously in what they reward, and no-boat reef diving call for specific topography, visibility, and operator infrastructure. These are the reefs we recommend most often for shore access, drawn from our own logbooks and from operator data across the four ocean basins. All appear on the [interactive map](/map).

## 1. Bonaire 1000 Steps

Predictable conditions and well-trained guides make this site a reliable starting point.

## 2. Eilat Coral Beach

A signature draw for the activity, with infrastructure to match.

## 3. Tulamben Liberty

Lower visitor pressure than the comparable headline destinations.

## 4. Bunaken shorelines

Excellent for the activity but only in the right season — plan accordingly.

## 5. Anse Chastanet St Lucia

A pinnacle of the genre; book early because demand outstrips supply.

## 6. Curacao Tugboat

A combination of accessibility and quality that punches above its weight.

## 7. Sharks Cove Oahu

Sheltered conditions on most days make this a robust fallback site.

## 8. Ningaloo Coral Bay

The site that defines the activity in this hemisphere.

## 9. Apo Island

A remote alternative for travellers willing to go the extra mile.

## 10. Aqaba Japanese Garden

Strong year-round and especially worthwhile on shoulder-season trips.
