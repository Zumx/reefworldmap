Thailand's reef diving is concentrated in two distinct regions: the Andaman
Sea on the country's western coast, with its deeper water, stronger currents,
and open-ocean encounters; and the Gulf of Thailand on the east, warmer and
shallower, best known for affordable training and the island reefs of Koh Tao
and Koh Lipe. Serious liveaboard diving in the Andaman reaches the Similan
and Surin Islands and, in the right season, Richelieu Rock. All sites are on
the [interactive map](/map).

## 1. Similan Islands National Park, Andaman Sea

The Similan Islands, eleven granite and coral islands 60 kilometres off the
Phang Nga coast, were closed to all visitors from May 2016 to October 2018 to
allow reef recovery after repeated bleaching and diver impact. They reopened
with a revised management framework including daily visitor quotas and
designated mooring sites. Hard coral recovery on the northern islands has been
documented by DMCR (Thailand's Department of Marine and Coastal Resources).
Best known sites include Fantasea, East of Eden, and Elephant Head Rock. Best
season: November to May.

## 2. Surin Islands National Park, Andaman Sea

The Surin Islands, more remote and less visited than the Similans, have been
similarly affected by bleaching but retain outstanding soft coral and fish
life. The Surin Islands are immediately adjacent to the Mergui Archipelago
in Myanmar and share its water quality and biodiversity. The Chao Lay
(Moken sea nomad) community has traditional fishing rights within the
national park, a governance arrangement unusual in Thai marine management.
Best season: November to April.

## 3. Hin Daeng and Hin Muang, Andaman Sea

Hin Daeng (Red Rock) and Hin Muang (Purple Rock) are submerged pinnacles in
deep water south of Koh Lanta, beyond the Phi Phi Islands. Hin Daeng is
encrusted with brilliant red and orange soft coral; Hin Muang, Thailand's
deepest known dive site, is completely covered in purple soft coral Alcyonaria sp.
from the top at four metres to beyond fifty. Manta rays Mobula alfredi and
Mobula birostris aggregate at cleaning stations on both sites. Best season:
November to April.

## 4. Richelieu Rock, Surin Islands

Richelieu Rock is a horseshoe-shaped submerged pinnacle near the Surin Islands
that is consistently rated among the top five dive sites in Southeast Asia.
Whale sharks Rhincodon typus visit reliably during their seasonal passage through
the Andaman (March to May). Harlequin ghost pipefish Solenostomus paradoxus,
seahorses, and frogfish Antennarius sp. are found in the soft coral; schooling
barracuda, snapper, and trevally aggregate over the top. Best season: March
to May.

## 5. Koh Lanta, Andaman Sea

The reefs around Koh Lanta — particularly on its southern point, Hin Daeng, and
on the eastern side's Shark Point (Hin Musang) — have recovered well from earlier
bleaching and now support productive reef communities. Leopard sharks Stegostoma
tigrinum rest on the sandy bottom at Shark Point, and giant moray eels Gymnothorax
javanicus are resident in crevices. Koh Lanta has a cluster of dive operators
with good liveaboard connections to the Similans. Best season: November to April.

## 6. Koh Phi Phi, Andaman Sea

Koh Phi Phi's Maya Bay was closed from 2018 to 2022 to allow recovery from the
reef destruction caused by sustained boat anchoring and extreme overcrowding
following its fame from the film The Beach. Reef health assessments since
reopening show significant hard coral recovery on the bay's walls, and visitor
management systems — limited daily entry, mooring-only anchoring — are now in
place. The broader Phi Phi reef has leopard sharks, turtles, and productive
soft coral walls on the southern islands. Best season: November to April.

## 7. Koh Tao, Gulf of Thailand

Koh Tao is Southeast Asia's primary dive training destination, producing more
PADI Open Water certifications annually than almost any other single location
in the world. Its gentle conditions, warm shallow water, and large cluster of
dive schools make it a logical starting point. The reef quality is fair rather
than exceptional — Sail Rock, a nearby pinnacle accessible by day trip, is the
best site in the area — but for a first reef experience combined with
certification, it delivers well. Best season: March to October.

## 8. Koh Lipe, Andaman Sea

Koh Lipe at the southernmost tip of the Thai Andaman coast is a small island
with fringing reefs and access to the reef systems of the adjacent Tarutao
Marine National Park, which has been less affected by bleaching than more
northern sites. Whale sharks Rhincodon typus pass through in season. The island
itself is a relaxed base combining beach holidays with productive reef snorkeling
and diving. Best season: November to April.

## 9. Koh Bida Nai and Koh Bida Nok, Phi Phi

The two Bida Islands south of Koh Phi Phi Don have some of the best hard coral
coverage in the immediate Phi Phi zone, with their southern faces having escaped
the worst anchor damage that affected shallower sites. Blacktip reef sharks
Carcharhinus melanopterus are regular on the reef flat; the walls have excellent
sea fans and barrel sponges. Both islands are accessible by day trip from Koh
Lanta and Phi Phi. Best season: November to April.

## 10. Anemone Reef, Andaman Sea

Anemone Reef, near the Similans, is a submerged pinnacle that takes its name
from the carpet of magnificent sea anemones Heteractis magnifica covering its
top section, along with their resident clownfish Amphiprion ocellaris. The
pinnacle structure concentrates schooling fish including fusiliers Caesio sp.
in large numbers, and the surrounding blue water regularly produces encounters
with manta rays and occasional whale sharks. Best season: November to May.
