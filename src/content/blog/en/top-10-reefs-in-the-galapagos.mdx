Galapagos sits at the centre of one of the planet's richest reef regions, a system shaped by Ecuador's volcanic archipelago. These ten reefs illustrate the breadth and quality of what divers and snorkellers can encounter here. All ten can be found on the [interactive map](/map).

## 1. Darwin's Arch

A signature site that delivers schooling pelagics and consistent visibility through the dry season. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 2. Wolf Island

Famous for its wall topography and dense soft-coral cover on the upper slope. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 3. Cousins Rock

A current-swept channel that produces some of the most reliable shark and trevally aggregations in the region. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 4. Gordon Rocks

A protected lagoon system with healthy hard-coral cover and good juvenile-fish density. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 5. Punta Vicente Roca

A drift dive along an outer reef edge with frequent megafauna passes. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 6. Cape Marshall

An accessible mid-shelf reef with strong recovery indicators after the most recent bleaching event. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 7. Floreana

A spawning aggregation site monitored by local marine scientists. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 8. Devil's Crown

A remote outer reef reached by liveaboard, with low diver pressure and pristine fish populations. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 9. Cape Douglas

A fringing reef that can be entered directly from a beach — rare for the region. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.

## 10. Roca Redonda

A cleaning-station site where mantas and reef sharks queue at predictable times of day. Best season varies; check local operators before booking.
