Indonesia sits at the apex of the Coral Triangle, the marine area with the
highest coral and reef fish species diversity on earth. The country's
17,000-island archipelago spans a vast range of ocean conditions — from the
warm, shallow Banda Sea to the cold upwellings of Komodo — and harbours reef
systems that range from current-swept pinnacles with oceanic predators to
sheltered macro-photography bays with bizarre invertebrate life. No single
country offers as much variety in reef diving. All sites are on the
[interactive map](/map).

## 1. Raja Ampat, West Papua

Raja Ampat encompasses the islands around Pulau Wayag and extends across the
Bird's Head Seascape, a protected area that contains the highest recorded marine
biodiversity of any reef system globally. A single dive at Cape Kri in 2001
produced a world record of 374 fish species identified in one dive. The soft
coral diversity is comparable to Fiji; the hard coral diversity exceeds anywhere
else on earth. Wobbegong sharks Orectolobus sp., walking sharks Hemiscyllium sp.,
and Papuan reef mantas Mobula sp. are endemic attractions. Best season: October
to April.

## 2. Komodo National Park, Nusa Tenggara

Komodo's reefs benefit from cold Indian Ocean upwellings that produce enormous
fish biomass. Crystal Rock and Castle Rock are submerged pinnacles exposed to
strong currents that concentrate schooling fish — bumphead parrotfish Bolbometopon
muricatum, dogtooth tuna Gymnosarda unicolor, grey reef sharks Carcharhinus
amblyrhynchos — while Manta Alley on the southern coast of Komodo Island is a
channel where resident oceanic mantas Mobula birostris can number twenty or more
individuals simultaneously. Best season: April to November for mantas.

## 3. Wakatobi National Park, Southeast Sulawesi

Wakatobi is a remote national park in the southeast of Sulawesi whose name is
an acronym of the four main islands: Wangi-wangi, Kaledupa, Tomia, and Binongko.
The reef system has exceptional hard coral diversity with minimal bleaching damage
attributable to the relatively cool, well-mixed waters of the Banda Sea approach.
The house reef at the single upmarket resort on Tomia Island is documented as one
of the best house reefs in the world. Rare invertebrates include nudibranchs of
extraordinary variety and the blue-ringed octopus Hapalochlaena sp. Best season:
April to November.

## 4. Bunaken National Park, North Sulawesi

Bunaken, near Manado, is one of Indonesia's oldest marine protected areas and was
protected before most of the country's conservation infrastructure existed. The
walls of Bunaken Island drop from three metres at the surface to over forty metres
in a single vertical face, colonised by enormous sea fans Gorgonia sp. and barrel
sponges Xestospongia testudinaria. Napoleon wrasse Cheilinus undulatus are
regular. The park's long protection has produced fish biomass significantly above
regional averages. Best season: May to October.

## 5. Lembeh Strait, North Sulawesi

The Lembeh Strait, a narrow channel between the mainland and Lembeh Island near
Bitung, is the world's most celebrated destination for muck diving — diving on
black volcanic sand and rubble seabeds to find bizarre, camouflaged, and
unusual invertebrate and fish life. Mimic octopus Thaumoctopus mimicus, frogfish
Antennarius sp. in multiple colour forms, flamboyant cuttlefish Metasepia pfefferi,
hairy frogfish, and mandarin dragonet Synchiropus splendidus on twilight dives
are signature finds. Coral reef diving is secondary here to the macro benthic
community. Best season: July to November.

## 6. Banda Sea, Maluku

The Banda Sea is the deep oceanic basin south of the Maluku Islands, reached by
liveaboard from Ambon or Sorong. Its seamounts — particularly around Banda
Neira and the volcanic Gunung Api — are nutrient-enriched and host some of the
most spectacular wall and blue-water diving in Southeast Asia. Schooling hammerheads
Sphyrna lewini, manta rays, and large pelagic fish are the draws. The historical
association with the Dutch spice trade adds land-based interest to what is
an outstanding dive destination. Best season: October to March.

## 7. Misool, Raja Ampat

Misool is the southernmost large island of Raja Ampat and is protected by a
no-take zone enforced by the Misool Foundation, which operates a resort within
the area. Fish biomass in the no-take zone is measurably higher than surrounding
areas; the recovery in shark and grouper populations since protection was
established in 2005 is one of the best-documented case studies in small-scale
MPA effectiveness. The underwater topography includes extraordinary mushroom
islands and cave systems. Best season: October to April.

## 8. Triton Bay, West Papua

Triton Bay is a remote fjord-like bay south of the Bird's Head peninsula, best
known for aggregations of whale sharks Rhincodon typus that gather around bagan
fishing platforms — traditional floating fish platforms — to feed on bycatch
thrown back into the water. The encounters are surface-accessible and year-round
but more common from October to April. The adjacent reef systems have good hard
coral cover and notable diversity of benthic invertebrates. Best season: October
to April.

## 9. Bali, Crystal Bay and Nusa Penida

Crystal Bay on Nusa Penida south of Bali is the primary site for encounters with
ocean sunfish Mola mola, which visit shallow cleaning stations between July and
October when cold water upwellings from the Indian Ocean bring their preferred
cool temperatures. Individual mola mola approaching three metres in height are
seen. Outside the mola season, the bay has good hard coral and large fish
populations. Strong currents make it unsuitable for beginners. Best season:
July to October for mola.

## 10. Alor, Nusa Tenggara

Alor is a remote island at the eastern end of Nusa Tenggara, two hours by
propeller plane from Bali, and one of Indonesia's least-visited serious dive
destinations. The currents between Alor and the adjacent small islands generate
spectacular fish life: schooling hammerheads, Napoleon wrasse, large grouper,
and an unusual diversity of pygmy seahorses Hippocampus bargibanti and
H. denise on sea fans. Liveaboard or small locally-run resort access. Best
season: May to November.
