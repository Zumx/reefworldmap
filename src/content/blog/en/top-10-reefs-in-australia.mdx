Australia holds more reef real estate than any other nation, from the tropical
northern Great Barrier Reef to the subtropical outposts of the Tasman Sea. Its
marine parks are among the most actively monitored in the world, and despite
severe bleaching events in 2016, 2017, and 2024, significant stretches remain
in outstanding condition. These ten sites represent the breadth and quality of
Australian reef diving. All are on the [interactive map](/map).

## 1. Great Barrier Reef, Queensland

The world's largest coral reef system extends 2,300 kilometres along Queensland's
coastline and encompasses roughly 2,900 individual reefs, from fringing reefs
around continental islands to isolated outer atolls. The variety of reef
morphologies and the species richness it supports — over 1,500 fish species,
400 coral species, and regular encounters with dwarf minke whales Balaenoptera
acutorostrata — make it a category of its own. Bleaching has been severe in the
northern sections; southern reefs around Lady Elliot and Heron Islands remain
in stronger condition. Best season: June to October.

## 2. Ningaloo Reef, Western Australia

Australia's other great reef system runs 260 kilometres along the remote
northwest coast of Western Australia, reaching close enough to shore for direct
beach snorkeling — a rarity for a world-class reef. Ningaloo is the best place
on earth for reliable whale shark Rhincodon typus encounters, concentrated
between March and July when annual coral spawning aggregates plankton. Manta
rays Mobula alfredi are present year-round at cleaning stations along the reef.
The adjacent Cape Range National Park adds landscape context to what is an
outstanding combined land-and-sea destination.

## 3. Cod Hole, Coral Sea (Ribbon Reef)

The Cod Hole is a specific site on Ribbon Reef Number 10 at the northern tip of
the Great Barrier Reef, accessible by liveaboard from Cairns. Its fame rests on
its resident population of potato cod Epinephelus tukula — massive grouper that
reach 1.5 metres in length and are so habituated to divers that they hover
centimetres away at depth. The site also regularly produces encounters with
dwarf minke whales, maori wrasse Cheilinus undulatus, and large reef shark
populations. A fringing reef section with extraordinary megafauna density. Best
season: June to August.

## 4. Osprey Reef, Coral Sea

Osprey Reef is an isolated oceanic atoll in the Coral Sea, 350 kilometres
northeast of Cairns and accessible only by liveaboard. Its North Horn site is
one of the most reliable locations in the world for close-range encounters with
whitetip reef sharks Triaenodon obesus, grey reef sharks Carcharhinus amblyrhynchos,
and silvertip sharks Carcharhinus albimarginatus. The walls of Osprey drop to
over 1,000 metres and are heavily colonised by large sea fans and black coral
trees. Oceanic conditions; suitable for experienced open-water divers. Best
season: August to November.

## 5. Heron Island, Queensland

Heron Island sits directly on the outer edge of the Great Barrier Reef's
southern section and is one of the few places where guests can wade directly
onto a reef flat at low tide. The coral bommies surrounding the island hold
dense populations of turtles — both green Chelonia mydas and loggerhead Caretta
caretta — and nesting colonies of black noddy terns. The reef wall on the
southern side has escaped severe bleaching that affected northern reefs and
remains one of the healthiest stretches of the entire system. Best season:
November to March for turtle nesting.

## 6. Lady Elliot Island, Queensland

The southernmost coral cay of the Great Barrier Reef, Lady Elliot has a
reputation as one of the system's best-recovered reefs after previous bleaching
events, benefiting from relatively cooler southern water temperatures. Manta
rays Mobula alfredi are the signature attraction, present year-round at cleaning
stations accessible from the island shore. The island is accessed only by light
aircraft from Bundaberg or the Gold Coast, which limits visitor numbers and
contributes to the reef's relatively pristine condition. Best season: May to
August for mantas.

## 7. Lizard Island, Queensland

Lizard Island is the most northerly of the Great Barrier Reef's resort islands
and was the epicentre of the 2016 bleaching event, which killed approximately
fifty percent of its shallow corals. Recovery has been ongoing, and recent
surveys show substantial juvenile coral recruitment in many areas. The Cod Hole
is reached by liveaboard from Lizard. The island's house reef is still
productive for turtles, reef fish, and night dives. Best season: June to
September.

## 8. Lord Howe Island, New South Wales

Lord Howe Island, 600 kilometres off the coast of New South Wales, hosts the
world's most southerly coral reef — a subtropical system that supports both
tropical and temperate species, producing an unusual mix. The corals here are
more heat-tolerant than their northern counterparts by some measures, having
adapted to more variable temperature regimes. Garibaldi fish Hypsypops
rubicundus appear alongside tropical species like butterfly fish. The island is
listed as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and visitor numbers are capped by
regulation. Best season: October to June.

## 9. Whitsunday Inner Reefs, Queensland

The fringing reefs around the Whitsunday Islands are more sheltered from the
bleaching that hit outer reefs, and the inner reef patches are highly accessible
by the large fleet of charter yachts and catamaran day trips operating from
Airlie Beach. Bait Reef and Hardy Reef have good hard coral cover and regular
populations of reef sharks, turtles, and schools of trevally Caranx sp. Best
season: May to October.

## 10. Reef HQ Aquarium, Townsville

Not a dive site, Reef HQ in Townsville is the world's largest living coral
reef aquarium and the education and conservation centre for the Great Barrier
Reef Marine Park Authority. For visitors who cannot dive or snorkel, or who
want to understand reef ecology before entering the water, it provides a unique
controlled encounter with a functioning reef ecosystem including reef sharks,
sea turtles, and a full range of coral morphologies. Best for: families, pre-trip
context-building, non-divers.
